Please structure the PRD with the following sections:

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- One-paragraph overview of the product/feature
- Key business impact and success metrics
- High-level timeline and resources needed

## 2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

- What specific problem are we solving?
- Who experiences this problem and how often?
- What is the impact of not solving this problem?
- Current alternatives and their limitations

## 3. GOALS & SUCCESS METRICS

- Primary objective (what success looks like)
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) with target numbers
- Secondary goals and nice-to-have outcomes
- How will we measure success 30/60/90 days post-launch?

## 4. USER PERSONAS & USE CASES

- Primary user persona (detailed profile)
- Secondary user personas
- Core user journeys and workflows
- Edge cases and less common scenarios

## 5. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

- Core features (must-have functionality)
- Feature specifications with acceptance criteria
- User interactions and expected behaviors
- Integration requirements with existing systems

## 6. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

- Performance requirements (speed, load times, etc.)
- Security and privacy considerations
- Scalability requirements
- Accessibility standards (WCAG compliance)
- Browser/device compatibility

## 7. USER EXPERIENCE (UX) SPECIFICATIONS

- Information architecture and navigation flow
- Key user interface elements and interactions
- Visual design guidelines and brand alignment
- Mobile responsiveness requirements
- Error states and edge case handling

## 8. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

- High-level architecture approach
- Required APIs and integrations
- Data model and storage requirements
- Third-party services needed
- Infrastructure and deployment considerations

## 9. CONSTRAINTS & ASSUMPTIONS

- Technical limitations
- Budget constraints
- Resource availability
- Regulatory or compliance requirements
- Dependencies on other teams/projects

## 10. TIMELINE & MILESTONES

- Phase 1: MVP delivery date and scope
- Phase 2: Enhanced features timeline
- Key milestones and deliverables
- Dependencies and risk mitigation

## 11. RISKS & MITIGATION

- Technical risks and mitigation strategies
- Business risks and contingency plans
- Resource risks and alternatives
- Market/competitive risks

## 12. SUCCESS CRITERIA & LAUNCH PLAN

- Definition of "done" for each phase
- Launch strategy and rollout plan
- Post-launch monitoring and iteration plan
- Success metrics tracking and reporting

**Writing Guidelines:**

- Be specific and measurable in all requirements
- Use clear, jargon-free language that stakeholders can understand
- Include concrete examples and user scenarios
- Prioritize features as P0 (critical), P1 (important), P2 (nice-to-have)
- Make assumptions explicit and call out dependencies
- Focus on the "what" and "why" rather than the "how"
